Safety

Siren protocol

  • You typically have ~15 minutes to reach shelter. The area is usually quiet, but sirens can occur.
  • During camp: everyone moves to the school shelter. All children are accompanied — no one goes home alone. If a parent prefers home sheltering, they must come pick their child up at the school.
  • Count every child against the day's attendance list, going in and coming out.
  • Typical stay is 15–20 min, occasionally up to 1 hr. Distraction kit (toys, face paint) lives in the shelter.
  • At the hotel (night alarms): go to the lobby. Alternative: underground parking shelter at nearby shopping center, 10-min walk.

Child pickup

  • Most kids arrive and leave on their own; parents bring the youngest.
  • Children aged 5–6: confirm they leave with a relative. When in doubt, ask our local partners — they know the families.

Border crossings

  • State clearly that we are doing humanitarian work; carry organization documentation.
  • Expect luggage checks — pack in an organized way; supplies are divided across bags (see the Supplies Checklist).
  • Let the Ukrainian citizens in the group speak for us where possible. Cross together.
  • All US team members register with the State Department (STEP) before departure.

Emergencies: alert the leads.

Lunch

  • Served in the school canteen, staggered by group across the lunch hour to avoid crowding.
  • Prepared by a local cook — hot, home-style Ukrainian cooking.
  • Staff eat the same catering, except those with food restrictions (3–4 people) who bring packed lunches from the hotel.
  • Water available at the school and at the Folkowisko center.
  • Training days: lunches at the hotel restaurant for staff and participants.

Menu: the final menu will be shared on WhatsApp.

Practical

Packing

  • Snacks — bars, easy things to carry
  • Raincoat
  • Coffee/energy items if you're particular
  • Power bank
  • Plug converter/adapter (Ukraine/Poland: type C/E, 230V)
  • Basic first aid

Phones & connectivity

We use Airalo eSIMs — they connect to a local network for data, calls, and texts. Buy and install before you leave the US; you can't test an eSIM until you land in the country it's for. They don't come with a local phone number, so make calls and texts over WhatsApp (or similar).

  • Poland: the shortest plan — 1 GB / 3 days (~$4).
  • Ukraine: the Unlimited plan matching your days in country (3, 5, 7, 10, 15 or 30 days).

Campfire reimburses every eSIM — Polish and Ukrainian. Save your receipts.

Receipts & expenses

Save every receipt for costs tied to the project days — travel or general (transport, cash payments, supplies, meals, eSIMs). Photograph each one and upload it to the Staff Receipts folder in real time — don't wait until you're home. This keeps the accountant's records clean and gets you reimbursed faster.
